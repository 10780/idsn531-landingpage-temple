My Story

I am a passionate technologist with a diverse background in various fields.


I started my technology career in 2019, my first startup was an ecommerce platform. I began this with two co-founders I befriended during undergrad.

I had done contractor work before then, but this was when things became much more serious as it related to a legitimate career.


In 2020, shortly after, I transitioned into hardware development, working on projects that combined software and physical devices.


One of biggest projects I worked on during this time in terms of significance was a medical device designed for epidemic prevention.

This was my first invention I could claim as mine entirely — I am the only person listed on the USPTO patent filing.

This was also the product of the second startup I was involved with, where I was the solo founder.


In 2023, I ended this operation.

Since then, I've been looking to license the technology I developed during this time.


After this, I began graduate studies at USC in Integrated Design, Business, and Technology.

I also took advantage of the university's networks and resources during this time to explore work in research commercialization.

In 2025, I began advisory work for a brain-computer interface spinout that originated at USC.

I also taught robotics, hardware prototyping, and programming to young students when I was a graduate student.


Outside of helping scientists become founders themselves, I have also done advisory work for more conventional startups from outside of academia.

One of these is for an AI-powered R&D tool for molecular discovery relating to exotic polymers.

I also wrote and published my first book in late 2024, and have written more about technology, business, and futures studies since then.


Currently, I am focused on completing my master's degree while continuing my advisory work.

I am also exploring new project ideas and potential collaborations in the tech space.


My Values

I believe in the power of technology to rewrite reality, as well as progress, disruption, and change for the sole sake of transformative growth.


I am committed to continuous learning and growth, both personally and professionally.

I believe strongly in long-term thinking, and have a deep nostalgia for the future.
