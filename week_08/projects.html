Project Highlights


Lunar Drone Project

This project involved the design and development of a drone capable of navigating the lunar surface.

The drone was designed to operate in low-gravity, low-atmosphere environments.

Its propulsion is handled by either ionic or cold gas thrusters.


Created for SF Deep Tech Week 2025.

Made in Solidworks.

Shout out to Blake for writing the white paper, Mohamed for making the presentation deck and having the idea, and Roxana for bringing our group together.


Whisper Terminal

The Whisper Terminal is a secure communication device designed for use in high-stakes environments.


Created for the Master of Science in Integrated Design, Business, and Technology program (Spring 2025).

Body designed in Solidworks. Circuit design and firmware coding was also done by me.

Powered by ESP32 wireless microcontroller.

Picture taken while still in development.


From Lab To Market, A Book on Research Commercialization and Spinouts

This book is a comprehensive guide to the process of research commercialization and the creation of spinout companies.

It covers topics such as intellectual property, funding, business models, and market analysis.

The book is intended for founder-curious research scientists and anyone interested in the commercialization of academic research.


Published just in time for Christmas 2024.

Available on Amazon in both paperback and Kindle formats.

A lot of study went into making this book.
